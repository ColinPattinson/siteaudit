SiteAudit

Experimental assistant to review public sector websites using the UK Government Service Standard, WCAG 2.2, privacy and security expectations, and Lighthouse‑style signals.

How this works

Enter a live URL and describe what the page is for and who it is for. The backend workflow (via n8n and an AI model) will fetch the page, compare it to your intent, and generate a mini report:

  • Overall 0–100 score (Lighthouse‑style banding)
  • Top 5 issues to address first
  • Selected UK Government Service Standard points (1, 3, 4, 5, 9)
  • WCAG 2.2 accessibility, privacy and security, SEO/performance, environment

How scores are intended to work

Overall score (0–100)

90–100: broadly good; smaller issues to address. 50–89: needs improvement; fix the top 5 issues first. 0–49: likely serious problems for users or compliance.

Service Standard

Selected points are scored out of 5 and given a red/amber/green rating in a way that loosely mirrors official Service Standard reports for digital services.

WCAG, privacy, SEO, environment

These sections highlight likely risks (for example, missing alt text, unclear cookie consent, very heavy pages) so teams can prioritise work alongside full accessibility and security reviews.

This is an experimental tool. Always run a full Service Standard assessment and accessibility audit for live public services.
